// 01 — about

Background

I have been interested in mobile robotics for as long as I can remember. In my sophomore year of high school I joined FIRST Robotics Competition Team 271, which sparked my passion for mobile autonomy.

I studied Computer Engineering at the University at Buffalo, became deeply involved in the Robotics Club and VEX team, and served as President of UB Robotics until graduating in 2021.

After two years in industry at Schneider Electric — working on image processing, OCR, sensor processing, and delta robot control — I returned to UB for a Master's in Robotics, focusing on off-road unstructured autonomy in the DRONES Lab.

Most recently I have worked hands-on with a FANUC LR Mate 200iD industrial arm and developed a novel CNN-based image processing product.
