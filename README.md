Robotics Engineer
-----------------

I have been interested in mobile robotics for as long as I can remember. In my sophomore year of high school, I joined my school’s FIRST Robotics Competition team, Team 271. This experience sparked my passion for robotics as a whole—and more specifically, for mobile autonomy.

I went on to study Computer Engineering at the University at Buffalo, where I became deeply involved in the Robotics Club and the VEX team. I later served as President of the UB Robotics Club, a role I held until graduating in 2021. Through these experiences, I developed both technical depth and leadership skills while working on hands-on robotic systems.

After graduating, I spent two years in industry growing into a well‑rounded engineer. During this time, I worked on image processing, optical character recognition, as well as sensor processing and control for a delta robot, contributing to a novel automation solution for validating HMIs at Schneider Electric. This project significantly strengthened my interest in robotics and reinforced my desire to work on complex autonomous systems.

Motivated by this experience, I returned to my alma mater to pursue a Master’s degree in Robotics. During my graduate studies, I focused on off‑road, unstructured autonomy, working on excavator automation research in the DRONES Lab at the University at Buffalo. This work deepened my understanding of perception, estimation, and control in challenging real‑world environments.

Over the past year, I have had the opportunity to work hands‑on with a FANUC LR Mate 200iD six‑axis industrial robot arm, while also developing a novel convolutional neural network–based image processing product at my current company. Together, these experiences reflect my continued passion for robotics—from autonomous mobile systems to industrial and intelligent automation.
